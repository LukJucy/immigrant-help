==============================================================
REFERENCE: Support, Rights & Helplines
==============================================================

PURPOSE
-------
Wellbeing, legal, and rights support services for students. Surface
these in a "Get help" section and within relevant guides (housing,
immigration, work).

MENTAL HEALTH & WELLBEING
  - Samaritans (24/7 freephone): 116 123 | https://www.samaritans.ie
  - Aware (depression & anxiety): 1800 80 48 48 | https://www.aware.ie
  - SpunOut (youth/student mental health): https://www.spunout.ie
  - Text About It (text "HELLO" to 50808): https://text50808.ie
  - HSE Your Mind Matters: https://www2.hse.ie/mental-health/
  NOTE: mental health support is a sensitive area. Present calmly,
  always offer the helpline, and avoid alarming language.

LEGAL & MIGRANT RIGHTS (free / low-cost)
  - Immigrant Council of Ireland (free legal advice for international
    students): https://www.immigrantcouncil.ie/rights/international-students
  - NASC - Migrant & Refugee Rights Centre (Cork-based):
    https://www.nasc.ie
  - FLAC - Free Legal Advice Centres: https://www.flac.ie
  - Citizens Information (everything about living in Ireland):
    https://www.citizensinformation.ie
  - An Garda Siochana (Irish police): https://www.garda.ie

HOUSING SUPPORT
  - Threshold (free tenancy advice): https://www.threshold.ie | 1800 454 454
  - Residential Tenancies Board (RTB): https://www.rtb.ie

WORK RIGHTS
  - Workplace Relations Commission (know your rights):
    https://www.workplacerelations.ie

STUDENT BODIES
  - Union of Students in Ireland (USI): https://usi.ie
  - Your college's own Students' Union (SU) - first stop for help

LAST VERIFIED: 8 June 2026 (source: user pre/after-arrival guides)
