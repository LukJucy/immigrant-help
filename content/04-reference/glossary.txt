==============================================================
REFERENCE: Glossary of Terms
==============================================================

PURPOSE
-------
Plain-English definitions of the acronyms and terms a new student
hits. The site shows these as tooltips/hover-cards on first use.

PPSN — Personal Public Service Number. Your unique ID for tax, social
  welfare, and public services. Needed to work and to be taxed
  correctly.

IRP — Irish Residence Permit. The physical card proving your legal
  permission to live in Ireland. Non-EEA students must register for it.

GNIB — Garda National Immigration Bureau. The old name people still
  use for immigration registration; now handled by ISD.

ISD — Immigration Service Delivery. The body that runs immigration
  registration and the appointment portal.

Stamp 2 — The immigration permission for full-time non-EEA students.
  Allows limited part-time work (term and holiday limits apply).

Stamp 1G — Permission that can apply to graduates staying to seek
  work after study.

Burgh Quay — The ISD Registration Office in Dublin city centre where
  first-time IRP registration appointments happen.

MyGovID — The Irish government's verified login. A basic account is
  the first step to using MyWelfare.

MyWelfare — The Department of Social Protection's online portal; where
  you apply for a PPSN.

myAccount — Revenue's online portal for PAYE/tax (register jobs,
  claim refunds, manage tax credits).

IBAN — International Bank Account Number. An Irish IBAN (issued by a
  licensed bank) is often required by employers and landlords.

PAYE — Pay As You Earn. The system where income tax is deducted from
  wages automatically.

USC — Universal Social Charge. A tax on income above a threshold;
  students under the threshold are exempt.

PRSI — Pay Related Social Insurance. Social insurance contributions
  deducted from pay.

Emergency tax — The high default tax rate applied when Revenue has no
  record of your job; avoided by registering the job in myAccount.

RPN — Revenue Payroll Notification. The instruction Revenue sends your
  employer with your tax credits; stops emergency tax.

Leap Card — The reusable smart card for public transport (bus, Luas,
  DART, rail). The Student Leap Card gives half-price fares.

TFI — Transport for Ireland. The national transport brand behind Leap.

Luas — Dublin's tram system. DART — the coastal rail line.

RTB — Residential Tenancies Board. Regulates tenancies and handles
  rental disputes.

SSA — Student Specific Accommodation. Purpose-built student housing,
  with its own tenancy rules.

Threshold — A charity giving free, independent advice to renters.

GP — General Practitioner (family doctor). Your first point of contact
  for non-emergency healthcare.

HSE — Health Service Executive. Ireland's public health service.

Stamp / permission renewal — Re-registering your IRP before it
  expires; insurance and other conditions are re-checked.

ILEP — Interim List of Eligible Programmes. The official list of
  courses that qualify for a student visa/Stamp 2. Your course MUST
  be on it.

AVATS — Irish Visa Application portal (visas.inis.gov.ie) where
  visa-required nationals apply for their student visa before travel.

D-type visa — Long-stay study visa for courses over 3 months (the
  main student visa). C-type — short-stay visa for courses under 3
  months.

Stamp 1G — Graduate permission allowing you to stay 12-24 months to
  seek work after finishing your degree.

EHIC — European Health Insurance Card. EU students get this in their
  home country for public healthcare access.

NDLS — National Driver Licence Service; where you exchange/apply for
  an Irish driving licence.

ISIC — International Student Identity Card; worldwide student
  discounts.

USI — Union of Students in Ireland; the national students' union.
