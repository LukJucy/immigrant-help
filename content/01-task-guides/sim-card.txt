==============================================================
TASK GUIDE: SIM Card / Mobile Plan
==============================================================

TASK:          Get an Irish mobile number / SIM
CATEGORY:      comms
PRIORITY:      Day 1 (do this first — you need a number for everything
               else: bank, IRP portal, appointments)
WHO NEEDS IT:  Everyone. An Irish number is needed for verification on
               most portals and services.
WHY:           Cheap data + an Irish number for 2FA, appointments,
               banking, and calls.

OPTIONS (prepaid / SIM-only — best value for students):
  - 48 (runs on Three's network): ~€12.99/mo, ~200GB data, all calls
    + texts, 5G included. Often cited as the cheapest 5G SIM-only
    plan. Free SIM order online. Value-focused, popular with students.
  - GoMo: ~€14.99/mo, unlimited domestic data (16GB roaming in
    EU/UK), unlimited domestic + EU/UK calls and texts. Simple flat
    pricing.
  - Three: most affordable big network; PAYG SIMs from ~€10, plans
    ~€15–25/mo. Strong value + coverage.
  - Vodafone / Clear Mobile: ~€14.99/mo unlimited (speed-capped on
    some plans). Widest coverage.
  - Eir: competitive mid-tier pricing, coverage focused on populated
    areas. | https://www.eir.ie
  - Tesco Mobile: https://www.tescomobile.ie
  Provider sites: 48 https://www.48.ie | Three https://www.three.ie |
  Vodafone https://n.vodafone.ie
  eSIM vs physical: most providers offer eSIM (instant) or a posted/
  in-store physical SIM.

STEPS:
  1. Compare plans on price, data, and roaming (esp. if you'll travel
     in the EU).
  2. Order online (eSIM or free posted SIM) or buy in a shop /
     supermarket / airport.
  3. Activate and (for value brands like 48) choose a membership/
     plan to start using it.
  4. Top up / set the monthly plan.

DOCUMENTS:
  - Usually just your passport. Most PREPAID SIMs do NOT require a
    PPSN or IRP. (Bill-pay/contract plans may need more — and usually
    aren't worth it for short stays.)

FEES:          ~€10–15 / month for a good prepaid plan
TIMEFRAME:     eSIM: minutes. Physical SIM: same day (shop) or a few
               days (posted).

OFFICIAL LINK:
  - https://gomo.ie/
  - Compare deals: https://goosed.ie/ (independent Irish comparison)
  [VERIFY current prices — mobile deals change often]

TIPS:
  - Get a prepaid/PAYG SIM, NOT a contract — no credit check, no
    lock-in, easy to switch.
  - Do this at the airport or day one so you have a number for every
    other task (bank, IRP portal need a phone number).
  - Check EU roaming caps if you plan to travel during breaks.
