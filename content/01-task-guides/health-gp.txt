TASK GUIDE: Health Insurance & Registering with a GP
==============================================================

TASK:          Sort health insurance + register with a doctor (GP)
CATEGORY:      health
PRIORITY:      Health insurance = PRE-ARRIVAL (needed for IRP/Stamp 2).
               GP registration = first few weeks.
WHO NEEDS IT:  All non-EEA students need private medical insurance for
               Stamp 2. Everyone should register with a GP.
WHY:           Insurance is a legal condition of your student
               permission and is checked at IRP registration/renewal.
               A GP is your first point of contact for healthcare.

HEALTH INSURANCE:
  - Non-EU students MUST have private health insurance for Stamp 2.
  - Cost: roughly €150–500 / year.
  - Must meet the minimum cover required for residency renewal:
    €25,000 for accident AND €25,000 for disease, including any
    hospitalisation. [VERIFY current minimum]
  - FIRST YEAR: travel/home-country insurance MAY suffice if it
    covers you for a full year (or your stay is under a year) and
    meets the €25,000 minimums.
  - AFTER first year: must buy insurance from an Ireland-based
    provider.
  - Compare plans (official tool): https://www.hia.ie
  - Major student insurers: Laya
    (https://www.laya.ie/health-insurance/students), VHI
    (https://www.vhi.ie/health-insurance/student-health-insurance),
    Irish Life Health (https://www.irishlifehealth.ie).
  - TIP: many colleges offer a GROUP student insurance plan that is
    cheaper than buying privately — check your college/SU first.
  - EU students: get your EHIC card before leaving home —
    https://www.hse.ie/eng/services/list/1/schemes/EHIC/

REGISTERING WITH A GP:
  STEPS:
    1. Ask your university health centre for nearby GP
       recommendations (most campuses have an on-site health centre).
    2. Or find a GP near you:
       - HSE find-a-GP: https://www.hse.ie/eng/services/list/2/gp/find-a-gp.html
       - Irish College of General Practitioners (ICGP): icgp.ie
       - GP visit card (free GP visits if low income): apply via
         mywelfare.ie
    3. Call/visit the practice to check they are accepting new
       patients.
    4. Complete their registration form and provide insurance details.
    5. Provide ID and proof of Irish address.

DOCUMENTS:
  - Photo ID (passport)
  - Proof of Irish address
  - Health insurance details
  - (For IRP) a valid insurance certificate meeting the minimums

FEES:          - Insurance: ~€150–500/year
               - GP visit: campus health centre ~€15–30; external GP
                 ~€50–70 per visit
TIMEFRAME:     Insurance: arrange before arrival. GP registration:
               same day–week once you pick a practice.

OFFICIAL LINK:
  - https://www.internationalstudents.ie/info-and-advice/immigration/health-insurance
  - https://www.tcd.ie/study/international/arriving-in-ireland/health-insurance/
  [VERIFY insurance minimums on irishimmigration.ie before launch]

TIPS:
  - Use the campus health centre — consultations are far cheaper
    than external GPs and often free for some services.
  - Keep your insurance certificate handy: you need it for IRP
    registration and renewal.
  - In an emergency, dial 112 or 999. A&E is for emergencies only.
