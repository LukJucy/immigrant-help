==============================================================
TASK GUIDE: Student Visa Application (before arrival)
==============================================================

TASK:          Apply for your Irish student visa (D-type / Stamp 2)
CATEGORY:      legal
PRIORITY:      PRE-ARRIVAL — apply well before your course start
WHO NEEDS IT:  Non-EU/EEA students from VISA-REQUIRED countries must
               apply BEFORE travelling. EU/EEA/Swiss: no visa, no IRP.
               Non-EU from NON-VISA-REQUIRED countries (e.g. USA,
               Canada, Australia, Brazil): no entry visa, but you MUST
               still register for IRP within 90 days (see
               irp-registration.txt).
WHY:           Legal permission to enter Ireland to study. Without it,
               visa-required nationals cannot board/enter.

DO YOU NEED A VISA?
  - Check your nationality:
    https://www.irishimmigration.ie/coming-to-study-in-ireland/
  - Student immigration FAQ:
    https://www.irishimmigration.ie/registering-your-immigration-permission/frequently-asked-questions-for-registration/

VISA TYPES:
  - C-type (Short Stay): courses UNDER 3 months. Single entry.
  - D-type (Long Stay Study): courses OVER 3 months — MOST STUDENTS
    NEED THIS. Fees: EUR 60 single entry / EUR 100 multiple entry.
    After arriving on a D visa, you register for Stamp 2 (IRP).

ELIGIBILITY (must meet ALL):
  1. COURSE: full-time, minimum 15 hours/week daytime classes, AND
     listed on the ILEP (Interim List of Eligible Programmes).
     Check ILEP first:
     https://www.irishimmigration.ie/coming-to-study-in-ireland/what-are-my-study-options/ilep-current-list/
     ILEP covers higher education (degrees/diplomas/masters), some
     English-language programmes, further education & training, and
     professional/vocational programmes.
  2. FINANCES (updated June 2025): prove IMMEDIATE ACCESS to:
       - EUR 10,000 for living costs PER YEAR of study (separate
         from tuition), OR
       - For 6-8 month courses: EUR 833/month (EUR 4,998 for 6 mo /
         EUR 6,665 for 8 mo)
       - PLUS proof of tuition fee payment (at least first semester)
     Accepted evidence: 6 months' bank statements; funds clearly
     accessible and under your control; no large unexplained
     deposits; credit cards NOT accepted; loan sanction letter /
     scholarship letter / sponsor docs + proof of relationship if
     applicable.
     Official update:
     https://www.irishimmigration.ie/reminder-on-student-finance-requirements-from-30-june-2025/
  3. ENGLISH LANGUAGE: valid certificate issued within 2 years of
     course start (one sitting; cannot combine dates):
       IELTS Academic: UG 6.0 (no band <5.5) / PG 6.5 / English 5.0
       TOEFL iBT:      UG 80 / PG 90-100 (check institution) / Eng 61
       PTE Academic:   UG 55 / PG 59 / English 30
       Duolingo:       accepted by many (not all) - check yours
  4. HEALTH INSURANCE: private cover valid in Ireland BEFORE applying;
     min EUR 25,000 accident AND EUR 25,000 illness/hospitalisation,
     for full duration of stay. See health-gp.txt.
  5. OTHER: passport valid 12+ months from arrival; good character
     (no serious criminal record); genuine student intent; intent to
     leave after studies; DISCLOSE any prior visa refusal (anywhere);
     explain any education gaps in writing.

HOW TO APPLY (AVATS):
  Portal: https://www.visas.inis.gov.ie
  1. Create an AVATS account.
  2. Complete the online form (match your passport exactly; choose
     "D Study Visa" if course > 3 months).
  3. Pay the fee online (EUR 60 single / EUR 100 multiple).
  4. Print and sign the summary form + declaration page.
  5. Gather required documents (see document-checklists.txt -> VISA).
  6. Submit physical documents to your nearest Irish Embassy/
     Consulate. Find it: https://www.ireland.ie/en/dfa/embassies/
  7. Wait for a decision: normally 4-8 weeks; peak season (Jun-Sep)
     up to 10-12 weeks. APPLY WELL IN ADVANCE.
  8. Travel to Ireland; keep your visa approval docs; register for
     IRP within 90 days.

COMMON REASONS FOR REFUSAL (avoid):
  - Funds below EUR 10,000 living costs
  - Tuition not paid before applying
  - Institution not on ILEP
  - Weak/vague cover letter or Statement of Purpose
  - No clear ties to home country
  - Unexplained education gaps
  - Prior visa refusals not disclosed
  - Medical insurance not valid in Ireland
  - Inconsistent information across documents

FEES:          C-type or D-type single EUR 60 / multiple EUR 100
TIMEFRAME:     4-8 weeks (up to 10-12 in peak season)

OFFICIAL LINK:
  - AVATS: https://www.visas.inis.gov.ie
  - Main portal: https://www.irishimmigration.ie
  - Citizens Information (student visas):
    https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/moving-country/moving-to-ireland/coming-to-live-in-ireland/student-visas/
  - Immigrant Council of Ireland (free advice):
    https://www.immigrantcouncil.ie/rights/international-students

TIPS:
  - Confirm your course is on the ILEP BEFORE paying anything.
  - Pay tuition (at least first semester) before applying — required.
  - Write a strong, specific Statement of Purpose.
  - Apply early; peak-season processing is slow.
