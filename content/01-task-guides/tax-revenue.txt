TASK GUIDE: Tax & Revenue (myAccount) — for working students
==============================================================

TASK:          Register your job with Revenue to avoid emergency tax
CATEGORY:      money / legal
PRIORITY:      Before your first day of paid work (needs PPSN first)
WHO NEEDS IT:  Any student who takes a part-time/holiday job. Stamp 2
               students can usually work limited hours.
WHY:           If Revenue has no record of your job, you are put on
               EMERGENCY TAX and lose a large chunk of your pay until
               it's fixed. Registering correctly prevents this.

STEPS:
  1. Get your PPSN first (see ppsn.txt) — you cannot register
     properly without it.
  2. Give your PPSN to your employer.
  3. Create a Revenue myAccount at revenue.ie.
  4. In myAccount: go to 'PAYE Services' -> 'Jobs and Pensions' ->
     'Add Job' and enter your employer's details. Do this BEFORE you
     start, or as soon as possible after.
  5. Revenue issues your employer a Revenue Payroll Notification
     (RPN) with your tax credits — this stops emergency tax.
  6. If you were already emergency-taxed, the overpayment is refunded
     automatically through payroll once the RPN is in place.

HOW EMERGENCY TAX HURTS:
  - With PPSN given: first 4 weeks taxed at 20% up to the standard
    band (40% above), NO tax credits; after week 4, all income at
    40%.
  - With NO PPSN given: ALL income taxed at 40% from day one.
  - USC and PRSI also apply at emergency rates.

GOOD NEWS FOR STUDENTS:
  - Most part-time students earning under ~€13,000/year pay little or
    no income tax, and are exempt from USC below that threshold; tax
    credits offset most of the rest. [VERIFY thresholds for current
    tax year]
  - National minimum wage (2025): €13.50/hour. [VERIFY current rate]

STAMP 2 WORK LIMITS (don't exceed):
  - Term time: MAX 20 hours/week
  - Holidays: MAX 40 hours/week (June, July, August, September, and
    15 December - 15 January)
  - Exceeding these violates your visa conditions.

CLAIMING A TAX REFUND (if over-taxed):
  - https://www.revenue.ie/en/jobs-and-pensions/tax-refunds/index.aspx

JOB SEARCH:
  - IrishJobs.ie | JobsIreland.ie (government) | ie.indeed.com |
    ie.linkedin.com
  - Know your rights: Workplace Relations Commission,
    https://www.workplacerelations.ie

DOCUMENTS / PREREQUISITES:
  - PPSN
  - Employer's registration details (they provide)
  - myAccount login (myGovID / myAccount)

FEES:          Free
TIMEFRAME:     Register before starting work; RPN issues quickly once
               the job is added.

OFFICIAL LINK:
  - https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/employment/starting-work-and-changing-job/starting-work/tax-and-starting-work/
  - revenue.ie -> myAccount -> PAYE Services
  [VERIFY rates/bands for the current tax year]

TIPS:
  - Register the job BEFORE your first payday — fixing emergency tax
    afterwards takes weeks.
  - Know your Stamp 2 work-hour limits (typically 20 hrs/week in
    term, 40 in holidays) — don't exceed them. [VERIFY]
  - Keep payslips; check you're getting your tax credits.
