==============================================================
TASK GUIDE: IRP (Irish Residence Permit) Registration
==============================================================

TASK:          Register your immigration permission (get your IRP card)
CATEGORY:      legal
PRIORITY:      First 90 days — HARD DEADLINE. Highest priority for
               non-EEA students.
WHO NEEDS IT:  All non-EEA/non-Swiss students staying longer than 90
               days. (EU/EEA/Swiss students do NOT need to register.)
WHY:           The IRP card is your legal proof of permission to live
               in Ireland (typically Stamp 2 for students). You must
               register to remain legally resident.

STEPS:
  1. Arrive in Ireland.
  2. Create a personal account on the ISD Customer Portal
     (Immigration Service Delivery).
  3. Book a first-time registration appointment online at
     inisonline.jahs.ie. Since Jan 2025, ALL first-time
     registrations are handled by ISD (no longer the Garda/GNIB).
     Slots open on a rolling basis up to ~10 weeks ahead.
  4. Upload/prepare your supporting documents (see below).
  5. Attend your appointment at the ISD Registration Office,
     13-14 Burgh Quay, Dublin 2, D02 XK70. (Outside Dublin:
     contact your local ISD office.)
  6. Pay the €300 registration fee.
  7. Your IRP card is printed centrally and POSTED to your address
     in ~10–15 working days (you do not get it on the day).

DOCUMENTS:
  - Passport (incl. biometric page) and entry visa where applicable
  - Evidence of the basis for permission: university admission/
    enrolment letter
  - Proof of address in Ireland
  - Proof of financial means (where required)
  - Private medical insurance (required for Stamp 2 — see
    health-gp.txt)

FEES:          €300 registration fee (unless in an exempt category)
TIMEFRAME:     - DEADLINE: register within 90 days of arrival
               - Appointment wait: officially 2–3 weeks, but real
                 Dublin waits were ~8–10 weeks in late 2025 [VERIFY]
               - Card posted ~10–15 working days after appointment
               - If you CANNOT get an appointment within 90 days, do
                 not panic: ISD will not cancel your permission while
                 you are waiting — but book as early as possible.

BOOKING PORTAL: https://inisonline.jahs.ie  (first-time registration)

OFFICIAL LINK:
  - https://www.irishimmigration.ie/registering-your-immigration-permission/information-on-registering/irish-residence-permit/
  - https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/moving-country/moving-to-ireland/rights-of-residence-in-ireland/registration-of-non-eea-nationals-in-ireland/

TIPS:
  - Book the appointment AS SOON AS you arrive — slots go fast and
    waits can eat most of your 90-day window.
  - Check the portal early in the morning; slots are released on a
    rolling basis.
  - Have the €300 ready and your insurance certificate sorted before
    the appointment.
  - Stamp 2 = student permission. Work limits: MAX 20 hrs/week
    during term; MAX 40 hrs/week during holidays (holiday periods:
    June, July, August, September, and 15 December - 15 January).
    Exceeding these limits violates your visa conditions.

STAMP 2 - WHAT IT ALLOWS:
  - Remain in Ireland for your course duration
  - Work up to 20 hrs/week (term) / 40 hrs/week (holidays)
  - Must be renewed each year (see RENEWAL below)
  - Permission stamps explained:
    https://www.irishimmigration.ie/registering-your-immigration-permission/information-on-registering/immigration-permission-stamps/

RENEWING YOUR IRP (each year):
  - Renew BEFORE your current card expires.
  - You can apply from 12 WEEKS before expiry — NOT earlier (earlier
    applications are refused).
  - Stamp 2 students: wait until your NEW course year starts before
    applying to renew.
  - All renewals are done ONLINE (not in person). Fee: €300/year.
  - You must be physically present in Ireland to renew.
  - Check live ISD processing times before applying:
    https://www.irishimmigration.ie/registering-your-immigration-permission/how-to-renew-your-current-permission/

TRAVELLING OUTSIDE IRELAND (re-entry):
  - To re-enter you need BOTH your valid passport AND your valid
    PHYSICAL IRP card. A photo/soft copy is NOT accepted.
  - Do NOT travel if your IRP card has expired - you may be refused
    re-entry.
  - If your renewal is pending, check the ISD site for any temporary
    travel arrangements before you travel.

AFTER GRADUATION - STAMP 1G (Graduate Programme):
  - Lets you stay to seek work without a job offer first.
  - Duration: 12 months (ordinary degree) / 24 months (postgrad,
    Level 9+).
  - Must apply before your Stamp 2 expires; must have graduated from
    an ILEP-listed programme; cannot work more than 40 hrs/week.
  - Dependents: only postgrad (Level 9+) students may bring a spouse/
    children, with extra financial means and separate applications.
  - Info: https://www.irishimmigration.ie/my-situation-has-changed-since-i-arrived-in-ireland/graduate-programme/
