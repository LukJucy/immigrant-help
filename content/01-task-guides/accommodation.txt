==============================================================
TASK GUIDE: Finding Accommodation & Tenant Rights
==============================================================

TASK:          Find housing in Dublin and know your renting rights
CATEGORY:      housing
PRIORITY:      PRE-ARRIVAL — and it unblocks PPSN and bank account
               (both need long-term address).
WHO NEEDS IT:  Everyone not in guaranteed university housing.
WHY:           Accommodation is the #1 stressor and the gateway task:
               you can't get a PPSN or easily open a bank account
               without a long-term address. Dublin's market is tight
               and expensive.

TYPES:
  - Student Specific Accommodation (SSA): purpose-built student
    housing, run by colleges or private companies. Term-time focused
    (roughly Sept–May). All SSA is under the RTB's remit.
  - Private rented: house/apartment shares or whole units on the
    open market.

KEY TENANT RIGHTS (verify current rules with RTB/Threshold):
  - DEPOSIT: a landlord can only ask for a deposit equal to 1
    month's rent.
  - RENT IN ADVANCE: you cannot be required to pay more than 1
    month's rent in advance to secure a tenancy.
  - SSA TERM: a student licence should not exceed 41 weeks unless
    you ask for longer; you can't be forced to pay for SSA over the
    summer if you don't need it.
  - NOTICE: students in SSA need give only 28 days' notice to end
    the arrangement; SSA providers must also give at least 28 days.
  - DISPUTES: any student can apply for RTB Dispute Resolution —
    even if the landlord never registered the tenancy.

INDICATIVE COSTS (2025/26):
  - Shared / basic room: from ~€800/month
  - Premium student rooms: €1,350+/month
  - One-bed apartment: ~€2,132 city centre / ~€1,872 suburbs
  [VERIFY — Dublin rents move fast]

STEPS:
  1. Apply for university/SSA housing as EARLY as possible (often
     before you've even accepted your offer).
  2. If renting privately, budget for 1 month deposit + 1 month rent
     up front (and no more).
  3. Get the tenancy/licence agreement IN WRITING.
  4. Never pay a deposit for a place you haven't verified is real
     (scam risk — see TIPS).
  5. Use your address to unblock PPSN and bank account.

DOCUMENTS:
  - ID (passport), university acceptance letter, references where
    asked, deposit + first month's rent.

WHERE TO SEARCH (listing sites):
  - On-campus/student housing: apply on your college website as soon
    as you accept your offer (search "[Your College] student
    accommodation")
  - Daft.ie (most popular in Ireland): https://www.daft.ie
  - MyHome.ie: https://www.myhome.ie
  - Student Accommodation Ireland: https://www.studentaccommodation.ie
  - CollegeSuite (purpose-built student housing): https://www.collegesuite.ie
  - Homes to Let: https://www.homesforrent.ie

OFFICIAL LINK:
  - https://rtb.ie/students/    (Residential Tenancies Board)
  - https://threshold.ie/faq/student-rental-rights/  (free advice)
  - https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/housing/renting-a-home/
  - https://dublin.ie/study/student-life/finding-student-accommodation/

TIPS:
  - SCAM WARNING: never transfer a deposit before viewing (or having
    someone trusted view) the property and confirming the landlord
    is genuine. This is the most common way students lose money.
  - Threshold gives free, independent tenancy advice — use it.
  - Start the search months early; book temporary accommodation for
    your first nights if needed (but remember temporary addresses
    don't work for PPSN).
