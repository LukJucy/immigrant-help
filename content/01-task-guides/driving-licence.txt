==============================================================
TASK GUIDE: Driving Licence (using a foreign licence)
==============================================================

TASK:          Use or convert your foreign driving licence
CATEGORY:      transport / legal
PRIORITY:      Only if you intend to drive; check before 12 months
WHO NEEDS IT:  Students who plan to drive in Ireland.
WHY:           Foreign licences are only valid for a limited time;
               non-EU licences may need conversion (or you must sit
               an Irish test) to keep driving legally.

KEY RULES:
  - EU/EEA licences: generally valid to drive in Ireland and can be
    exchanged for an Irish licence.
  - Non-EU/EEA licences: may be used for a limited period (commonly
    up to 12 months). After that you may need to convert (if Ireland
    has an exchange agreement with your country) OR pass the Irish
    driver theory + practical test.
  - Whether you can EXCHANGE depends on your country having an
    agreement with Ireland — check the official link for your
    specific country.

STEPS:
  1. Check if your country has a licence-exchange agreement with
     Ireland (Citizens Information link below).
  2. If yes: apply to exchange via the NDLS (National Driver Licence
     Service). You'll typically need ID, proof of address, PPSN, and
     your current licence.
  3. If no: you must sit the Driver Theory Test, get a learner
     permit, and pass the driving test to drive long-term.

OFFICIAL LINK:
  - Citizens Information - using a foreign licence in Ireland:
    https://www.citizensinformation.ie/en/travel-and-recreation/motoring/driver-licensing/

TIPS:
  - Don't let your foreign licence "run out" of validity here —
    check the 12-month rule for your country early.
  - In Dublin, public transport + Leap card usually beats driving;
    you may not need this at all.
