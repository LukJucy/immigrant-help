==============================================================
TASK GUIDE: Open a Student Bank Account
==============================================================

TASK:          Open an Irish bank account
CATEGORY:      money
PRIORITY:      Week 1–2 (can START pre-arrival with some banks)
WHO NEEDS IT:  Any student who wants an Irish IBAN for rent, wages,
               bills, and day-to-day spending.
WHY:           Many landlords and employers need an Irish IBAN. Only
               licensed banks issue Irish IBANs; some employers won't
               accept non-Irish IBANs.

OPTIONS:
  TRADITIONAL (licensed, Irish IBAN; usually need PPSN + Irish
  address — apply after arrival):
    - AIB Student Account — zero fees | https://aib.ie/students
    - Bank of Ireland (BOI) student account — no maintenance/
      transaction fees, contactless card, branches on many campuses
      | https://www.bankofireland.com/personal/current-accounts/student-account/
    - Permanent TSB student account |
      https://www.permanenttsb.ie/current-accounts/explore-current-accounts/student/
    - AIB & BOI let you START the application online before arrival
      (BOI "Coming to Ireland", up to ~45 days before arrival)
  DIGITAL (fast, no PPSN needed, check IBAN/employer acceptance):
    - Revolut — licensed/regulated (Bank of Lithuania), provides an
      Irish IBAN, currency exchange, physical + virtual cards,
      Apple Pay. Opens in minutes. | https://www.revolut.com/en-IE/
    - N26 — can open remotely with home-country details, no PPSN
      required. | https://n26.com/en-ie
    NOTE: confirm a digital account's IBAN is accepted by your
    employer/landlord before relying on it as your main account.

STEPS:
  1. Choose traditional (full service, campus branches) and/or
     digital (instant, good as a backup).
  2. For BOI/AIB: start the application online; some steps can be
     done pre-arrival.
  3. Gather documents (below).
  4. Complete the application — in branch (often during orientation
     week) or in-app for digital banks.
  5. Receive your IBAN and debit card.

DOCUMENTS:
  - Passport
  - IRP or visa
  - University acceptance/enrolment letter
  - Proof of Irish address (tenancy agreement or utility bill)
  - Some banks may also ask for PPSN or a college Letter of
    Introduction

FEES:          AIB Student Plus & BOI student accounts: no fees
TIMEFRAME:     - Traditional: 1–3 business days (often immediate
                 during orientation periods)
               - Digital (Revolut): working account in minutes,
                 full validation 1–2 days

OFFICIAL LINK:
  - https://aib.ie/our-products/current-accounts/student-plus-account
  - https://www.educationinireland.com/en/living-in-ireland/banking-in-ireland

TIPS:
  - Proof of Irish address is the usual sticky point — a college
    "Letter of Introduction" can help if you don't have a utility
    bill yet.
  - Open a digital account (Revolut/N26) BEFORE arrival so you can
    spend day one, then add a traditional account for the Irish IBAN.
  - Orientation week is the easiest time — banks set up stalls on
    campus.
